When the user says "critically review the plan", they want me to find more issues, not just present the already-reviewed plan. Each review round should find NEW problems or validate that previous issues are resolved. Don't try to exit plan mode until the user is satisfied — they'll tell me when to proceed.

**Why:** The user has consistently asked for 2-3 rounds of critical review before approving plans. The first review catches obvious issues, but subsequent reviews should dig deeper into assumptions, edge cases, and realistic feasibility.

**How to apply:** After each "critically review" request, re-read the plan with fresh eyes and look for: incorrect assumptions, missing edge cases, unrealistic effort estimates, unnecessary complexity, things that could be simpler. Don't just restate previous findings.
